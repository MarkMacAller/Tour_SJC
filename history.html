It started with the Southern Exhibition 1883-1887

The grounds of the southern exhibition coverd what is now St. James Court as well as surrounding area. The area included between current 4th and 6th streets from Park Ave. to Hill Streets. The building housing the exhibition contained thirteen acres under roof. Following the close of the exhibition, there was little use for the building or surrounding grounds so the building was demolished and St James Court was born.
